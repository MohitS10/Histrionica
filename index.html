Aahvaan

Prepare to have your energy levels soar as the beat of the dafli thrums through your veins and reverberates through the massive crowd assembled under the shade of our famous banyan tree. Loud and clear voices ringing in the air, their exuberance silencing the crowd with every shout. Expect teams to contort beyond expectation creating human formations that will keep you spellbound. With applications from over fifty colleges, Aahvaan is a magnificent blast of life, music, color and grandeur all coming together to raise pressing issues in the society we live in, leaving the audience in splits and more often than not, in tears.


General Instructions:
1. All the members participating in the event must be college students carrying their official college identity cards.
2. After a script has been selected, a confirmation e-mail bearing all necessary instructions will be sent to the teams expected to appear for their respective preliminary rounds on the allotted date and time.
3. Time Limit: 10 minutes

Charades

Charades, the bilingual stage play competition, has been leaving the audience entranced since its inception and has attracted theatre lovers from across 30 colleges across the country. The magnitude of every performance is amplified by the audience of over a thousand that assembles in the college auditorium. This event, of national repute, has now come to represent the best of college theatre.


General Instructions:
1. There may be a maximum of two entries from each college for Charades.
2. In case of two entries from the same college, it is highly recommended that the teams perform plays of different languages. If not adhered to, it may impact the selection criteria.
3. All the members participating in the event must be college students carrying their official college identity cards.
4. Lighting of cigarettes, matchsticks and candles is hazardous on stage and will lead to disqualification.
5. After a script has been selected, a confirmation e-mail bearing all necessary instructions will be sent to the teams expected to appear for their respective preliminary rounds on the allotted date and time.
6. Time Limit: 10 minutes (Including stage set up and clearance)
